Frequently Asked Questions

  • What type of cars do you restore?
    I specialize in restoring classic Minis, the original ones—mainly from the 1960s to the early 2000s. The classic Mini is a real passion of mine, and there's nothing quite like bringing one of these little cars back to life. That said, I also occasionally work on other classic British cars, like the Morris Minor or Triumph Spitfire, but my real bread and butter is the Mini. Whether it’s an engine rebuild, rust treatment, or a full restoration, I’ve got a soft spot for those quirky little cars. They might be small, but they’ve got character and history in spades.
  • How long does a typical mini restoring take?
    The time it takes to restore a classic Mini really depends on the condition of the car when it comes in, as well as the level of restoration the owner is after. A basic restoration, where we’re focusing on getting the car running well and cleaning it up, might take around 4 to 6 months. But if it's a full-on, ground-up restoration—taking care of rust, rebuilding the engine, redoing the interior, and really bringing it back to showroom condition—that can easily take 9 months to a year, or even longer in some cases. We don’t rush the process, though. Every Mini deserves the proper attention, and it's all about getting the details right. Sometimes we end up waiting on parts or doing a bit of custom work, so timelines can shift a bit, but in the end, it’s always worth it to see that Mini driving down the road like it’s fresh off the production line
  • What is the cost of restoring a mini?
    For a basic restoration, where we're doing things like fixing mechanical issues, cleaning up the body, and addressing any minor rust, you're probably looking at somewhere between £5,000 to £8,000. That would include things like engine work, suspension refurbishing, and a solid paint job. However, if you’re going for a full, nut-and-bolt restoration—where everything from the body shell, engine, and gearbox to the interior is stripped down and rebuilt—you could be looking at anywhere from £10,000 to £20,000 or more. Concours-level restorations, where every part is either replaced or meticulously restored to its original specification, could even exceed £25,000, especially if you're using genuine or rare parts.The main drivers of the cost are the condition of the car, the parts needed (some parts for these old Minis can be hard to find or expensive), and how much custom work is involved. Labour is another factor, as I put a lot of hours into getting everything just right.It’s definitely an investment, but when you're driving a beautifully restored Mini, it’s hard to put a price on the joy of it!
  • Do you handle bodywork and paint?
    Yes, I definitely handle all aspects of bodywork and paint. In fact, I’d say it’s one of the most rewarding parts of restoring a classic Mini. When you’ve got a car that’s over 40 or 50 years old, the body can often be in pretty rough shape—rust, dents, and general wear-and-tear are pretty common.
  • Can I supply my own parts for the restoration?
    Absolutely, you can supply your own parts for the restoration! I’m more than happy to work with parts that you provide, whether it’s for the engine, bodywork, suspension, or anything else. Some owners prefer sourcing their own parts for a variety of reasons—maybe you’ve found a rare original part, or you’ve got specific preferences for performance upgrades.
  • Do you offer any warranties or guarantees on your work?
    Yes, I do offer warranties and guarantees on the work I do. I’m committed to quality and making sure you’re happy with the final result, so I stand behind the restoration and modifications we carry out on your Mini. For most of the restoration work, whether it’s mechanical, bodywork, or paint, I offer a 12-month workmanship guarantee. This means that if any issues arise due to the way something was installed or done during the restoration I’ll take care of it at no extra cost to you within that time frame.
  • Do you offer custom modifications for rally or performance mini's?
    Absolutely, I do! When it comes to building Minis for rallying or high-performance driving, I’ve got a lot of experience with custom modifications to make these little machines not just roadworthy, but race-ready.For rally Minis, we focus on performance, durability, and safety. I can set up the engine for more power, fine-tune the suspension for better handling on rough terrain, and install stronger braking systems. Whether it’s a stage rally Mini that needs lightweight body panels and a roll cage, or a road rally setup that needs a perfect balance between speed and comfort, I can help with that.
  • Do you organise or participate in classic car rallies?
    Yes, absolutely! I do both organize and participate in classic car rallies, especially when it comes to Mini-specific events. I’ve always been passionate about getting classic Minis out on the road and giving them a chance to shine in the spotlight. There’s something really special about seeing a whole bunch of these iconic little cars together, whether it’s for a casual meet-up or a more competitive rally.

Newsletter

Through his monthly newsletter, Jack shares his expertise and insights with a growing community of readers who are either working on their own restoration projects or simply appreciate the art of bringing old cars back to life. In each issue, Jack offers a blend of practical advice and personal stories, making the newsletter a valuable resource for both experienced restorers and newcomers. The newsletter includes step-by-step guides on tackling common challenges in car restoration, from rust removal to engine rebuilding, as well as tips on sourcing rare or original parts. Readers can look forward to articles that dive deep into the specifics of restoring different parts of a car, whether it's bodywork, upholstery, or mechanical systems.A unique feature of the newsletter is its focus on classic Minis, but Jack doesn't limit his knowledge to just these iconic cars. While his business specializes in Minis, he regularly offers general restoration tips that can be applied to a wide range of vintage vehicles. This makes his newsletter a great read for anyone restoring a classic car, regardless of make or model. Jack's expertise is evident in every issue, and his readers appreciate the practical, no-nonsense approach he takes to solving complex restoration problems.Additionally, Jack’s newsletter often highlights ongoing projects at Jack’s Minis, providing updates on the vehicles currently being restored in the shop. These stories not only show the dedication behind each restoration but also provide readers with real-world examples of how different restoration techniques are used, as well as before-and-after photos, giving readers a sense of the transformative power of a well-executed restoration.
