Current Projects


A rare chance to both restore a car, respray it to a new colour, as well as adding rally enancements to it. Projects like these are what i enjoy the most, the challenge it brings is fun. Restoration and Rally Enhancements Tasks:
Body and Paintwork:
Rust Removal:

Sanding down and removing all visible rust from the body panels, wheel arches, and door sills. Treating rust-prone areas with rust inhibitors and protective coatings to prevent recurrence.
Panel Repair or Replacement:

Repairing or replacing heavily corroded sections of metal. Welding and shaping new panels to restore structural integrity.
Respray:

Applying primer coats followed by a rally-style paint job with sponsor decals, racing stripes, or a bold color scheme (e.g., white body with black stripes or British racing green with white accents).
Interior Modifications:
Rally-Ready Cockpit:
Installing a roll cage for added safety during rally events. Replacing the standard seats with lightweight, FIA-approved bucket racing seats. Adding a 5-point harness system for driver and passenger. Installing a quick-release steering wheel for enhanced control.
Performance Upgrades:
Engine Modifications:

Installing performance upgrades such as a high-performance air intake, tuned exhaust system, and a lightweight flywheel. Optimizing the engine tuning for increased horsepower and torque.
Suspension and Brakes:

Installing rally-tuned shock absorbers and coilovers for better handling on uneven terrain. Upgrading the brakes with high-performance rotors and calipers to improve stopping power during high-speed stages.

Engine Replacement:
Removing the old engine and safely disconnecting all components (e.g., exhaust manifold, coolant lines, and electrical harnesses). Installing a new, fully rebuilt engine compatible with the original drivetrain specifications. Replacing or upgrading ancillary components like the air intake, radiator, and alternator.
Fine Tuning:
Testing and tuning the new engine to ensure optimal performance and fuel efficiency. Ensuring that all systems (ignition, cooling, and fuel) work cohesively with the replacement engine.
